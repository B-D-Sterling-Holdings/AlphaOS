# Position Sizing Framework

## Overview

Position sizing is the systematic process of determining how much capital to allocate
to each investment opportunity. Proper position sizing is essential for managing
portfolio risk and maximizing risk-adjusted returns.

## Core Principles

1. **No single position should exceed 10% of portfolio**
2. **Higher conviction warrants larger positions**
3. **Higher risk requires smaller positions**
4. **Sector concentration should be limited**
5. **Always maintain minimum 0.5% position threshold**

## Conviction-Based Base Allocation

### Conviction Levels

#### Very High Conviction (Base: 12-15%)
Most of the following must be true:
- Trading at >20% discount to conservative intrinsic value estimate
- Multiple positive catalysts expected in next 6-12 months
- Strong competitive moat with clear, verifiable evidence
- Great fundumental profile
- Expected return over 15%

#### High Conviction (Base: 7-12%)
THREE of the four criteria above must be true, OR:
- Trading at >15 discount with strong fundamentals
- Established competitive position
- Good fundumentals
- Expected return over 12%

#### Moderate Conviction (Base: 3-7%)
Most of these are true (and havnt selected a high or very high conviction already):
- Thesis intact but quite a lot of near-term uncertainty
- Good business with average positioning
- Expected return over 10%

#### Low Conviction (Base: 0-2%)
- Speculative opportunity
- High uncertainty on key variables
- Thesis requires specific events to play out
- Consider whether position is worth monitoring overhead


## Documentation Requirements

For each position, document:
1. Target position size and rationale
2. Entry price and date
3. Thesis summary
4. Key metrics to monitor
5. Stop-loss level
6. Price target(s)
7. Review trigger events
